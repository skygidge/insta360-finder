**Location:** Kyiv, Ukraine  
**Product:** Insta360 Titan  
**Source:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12016277/

**Story summary:**  
Research psychologist Olga Kukharuk of the Institute of Social and Political Psychology (Ukrainian National Academy of Pedagogical Sciences) created immersive 360-degree VR therapeutic content filmed with an Insta360 Titan camera for Ukrainian war veterans. The study — published in the *European Journal of Psychotraumatology* in April 2025 (PMC12016277) — demonstrated statistically significant reductions in anxiety (up to 14.5%) and depression (up to 12.3%). ## Learnings

### What worked
- **Conservation diving publications** (Scubaverse) are a productive niche source — professionals in underwater filmmaking often write up their expeditions with gear details
- **Academic databases** (PubMed/PMC) surface unexpected professional Insta360 use in medical/therapeutic research — search terms like `"Insta360" site:pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov` or `"Insta360" "360 camera" VR therapy` work well
- **Fulldome Database (fddb.org)** confirmed film completion status when the project's own website was ambiguous — useful verification layer for dome/planetarium projects
- Combining `"Insta360" + niche domain` (e.g., fulldome, VR therapy, shark conservation) surfaces stories mainstream searches miss

### What didn't work
- Standard Week C platforms (DPReview forums, Vimeo, No Film School, Filmmaker Magazine) yielded nothing new this run — likely saturated or low Insta360 mention rate in those communities
- Bot-detection (403) blocked many source links; mitigated by requiring 3+ independent search snippets before including a story

- `fddb.org` — Fulldome Database, useful for verifying completion of dome/planetarium films

### Patterns noticed
- Academic use of Insta360 (medical, psychological, research) is an underexplored category — the brand appears in peer-reviewed methods sections
- Humanitarian/conservation use cases tend to have higher editorial value and are rarer than adventure/travel stories
- Week C searches benefit from combining standard platform searches with targeted niche domain searches
